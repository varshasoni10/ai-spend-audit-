# Go-To-Market Plan

## Target User
The target user is the "Head of Engineering" or "CTO" at a Series A/B startup (20-100 employees). They just got their AWS/SaaS bill for the month and are feeling the pressure from their finance team to optimize burn, but they don't have time to do a vendor-by-vendor analysis themselves.

## The "Right Before" Moment
They just searched "How to manage ChatGPT Enterprise licenses" or "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot pricing for teams" on Google, or they were scrolling through a Hacker News thread complaining about SaaS seat bloat.

## Where They Hang Out
- Specific Subreddits: r/SaaS, r/startups, r/devops, r/ExperiencedDevs
- Slack/Discord: CTO Craft Slack, YC Alumni Slack, local tech leadership groups.
- X Lists: VCs, bootstrapped founders, engineering leaders.

## First 100 Users ($0 Budget)
1. **The "Show HN" Launch**: Launch on Hacker News as a free tool ("Show HN: I built a tool to show startups how much they overpay for AI"). The audience naturally hates SaaS bloat and loves optimization.
2. **Cold Emailing Recent Fundraises**: Scrape recent Series A announcements. Cold email the CTO: "Congrats on the round. We built a free tool to make sure your new runway isn't wasted on AI tool seat bloat. Takes 30 seconds."
3. **Open Source/Indie Hacker Communities**: Post in indie hacker forums sharing the data of "What the average startup wastes on AI tools" (using anonymized early data).

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex has existing relationships with startups who already bought discounted credits. Credex can email their existing user base: "Check if you're optimizing your other AI tools" which creates a viral loop since the results are highly shareable ("I just saved $5k/yr").

## Week-1 Traction
- 500 Unique Visitors
- 150 Audits Completed (30% conversion on form)
- 40 Emails Captured (26% capture rate on results)
- 5 High-Intent Leads booking a consultation with Credex.
